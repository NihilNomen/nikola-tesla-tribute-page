Nikola Tesla

The Man who Usher the Age of Electricity

Nikola Tesla


Timeline of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • July 10, 1856: Nikola Tesla is born in Smiljin, Austrian Empire

  • 1873: Shortly returning to his home town, his contracts cholera and is bedridden for nine months and near-death many times. Tesla's father, in a moment of desperation, agreed to send him to an engineering school if he recovered from the illness.

  • September 1875: Tesla enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic School in Graz on a Military Border scholarship. During his first year, Tesla never missed a lecture, made highest grades possible and started a Serbian culture club. At the end of the second year, Tesla lost his scholarship and took up gambling. Near the end of the third year, Tesla stopped attending lectures and dropped out the following year.

  • April 17, 1879: Tesla's father, Milutin Tesla contracts an unspecified illness and dies at 60

  • January 1880: Tesla's uncles, Petar and Pavle, pay for his travel to Prague, but he arrives too late to enroll in Karl-Ferdinand University, and resorts to audits letures.

  • 1881: Tesla moves to Budapest and works for Central Telegraph Office making numerous improvements and developed an amplifer

  • Feburary 1882: Tesla, obsessed with solving the riddle of A.C. suffers a mental breakdown; after recovering a short time later the solution comes to him in a vision during a walk in the Varosliget city park of Budapest with friend Anthony Szigety.Tesla looking at the setting sun and recited a passage from Goethe's Faust. At that very moment the idea came to him in a flash and he drew a diagram of the motor in the sand with a stick.

  • April 1882: Tesla works for the Continental Edison Company, while there he develops plans to improve the Edison dynamos into the automatic regulator, but is dispatched for Strasbourg, before his recieved his payment

  • June 10, 1883: Tesla demonstrates the completed A.C. induction motor to Mr. Bauzin, former mayor of Strasbourg, and several potential investors; while interested, it is clear they do not understand its value

  • June 6, 1884: Tesla arrives in New York, after surviving rough trip in which his ticket, money and some of his luggage were stolen, and the ship had muntiny.

  • June 8, 1884: Edison hired Tesla on the spot but would dismissed A.C. power system. Soon after he was dispatched to Henry Villard's ocean liner, the Oregon, the first boat ever to have electric lighting. The Oregon's dynamos powering the ship were badly damaged and Edison amazed to learn that Tesla had repaired them, quoted Tesla "a damn good man." While working for Edison he also installed and repaired incandescent and arc lamps, reassembled D.C. generators and designed twenty-four different types of machines that became standards replacing original Edison designs.

  • March 1885: Investors ask Tesla to develop an arc lighting system, Tesla starts the Tesla Electric Light Company in Rahway, New Jersey. Soon after completing it, he is forced out of the company leaving him with worthless stock certificates. Tesla later referred to this as "the hardest blow" he'd ever received.

  • 1885: At some point during Tesla's employment, Edison promises to pay him $50,000 if he improved the performance of Edison's D.C. dynamo. Tesla succeeds beyond Edison's expectations, but Edison reneges on his promise to pay Tesla. Edison is said to have quoted, "You don't understand our American humor." Deeply hurt, Tesla resigns. Some accounts state that Edison offers Tesla a raise if he will reconsider but Tesla declined.

  • 1886: Betrayed by men he trusted, Tesla considered the winter of 1886/1887 a time of "terrible headaches and bitter tears." During the time he work as a ditch digger for $2.00 per day, questioning the value of his education.

  • April 1887: Tesla was introduced to Alfred S. Brown, director of Western Union, and Charles F. Peck, a New York City attorney, who were sold on his alternating current system after he demonstrated his "Egg of Columbus." Tesla agreed to split his patents fifty-fifty in exchange for funding. Brown located the laboratory at 89 Liberty St., the company filed for its first patent by the end of the month.

  • July 7, 1888: Tesla sells patents for A.C. Polyphase System to George Westinghouse for $25,000 in cash, $50,000 in notes and a royalty of $2.50 per horsepower for each motor. He later moves to Pittsburgh to help develop the motor for a year

  • 1891: Tesla Patents the Tesla Coil, a wireless lighting system

  • July 30, 1891: Tesla is naturalized as a citizen of the United States

  • 1893: Tesla and George Westinghouse power the World's Columbian Exposition with AC Electricity, Tesla showcases the "Egg of Columbus" at the exposition

  • 1901: Construction of Tesla's Wardenclyfee Tower bns, the Wardenclyfee Tower was intended to provide free wireless electricity

  • 1913: Tesla patents the bladeless turbine

  • 1915: Tesla and Edison are ignored by the Nobel Prize Committee given animosity toward each other neither was ever given the award.

  • August 4, 1915: Tesla files a lawsuit against Guglielmo Marconi for infringement of radio patent number 645,576. This patent was later given posthumorously to Nikola Tesla in 1943, when the Marconi Company sue the United States Government for using it during World War I

  • July 10, 1934: Tesla particle beam research is publicized by the New York Times

  • January 7, 1943: Nikola Tesla dies of heart failure in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel
